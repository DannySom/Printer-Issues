This is a quick and simple repository to reference whenever you come across printer issues in the real world. It will cover the most common issues you may encounter in classrooms, offices, workplaces, etc.

<h2>Lines down the printed pages</h2>

**Possible Causes or Root Issues:** Most likely, on the photosensitive drums, it may be scratched or sometimes, it may get dirty with grease or debris


<ins>Fixes:</ins> 

**1.)** Reset the printer.

**2.)** Take off the drum and locate the dirt or debris. If you have an inkjet printer, inspect the print heads.

**3.)** Clean the drum using a soft cloth and distilled water. You can also use isopropyl alcohol.

**4.)** If the drum is permanently damaged, you may need to replace the entire drum

 <h2>Paper Jams</h2>

**Possible Causes or Root Issues:** A lot of times, rollers may get worned out over time as it is made of rubber.


<ins>Fixes:</ins> 

Check all rollers and replace wormed ones. Ensure you have the right type of paper. Not all printers can handle the thinness of the papers.

 <h2>Faded Print</h2>

**Possible Causes or Root Issues:** Obstructed rollers, misaligned paper, paper scraps, or poor paper quality

<ins>Fixes:</ins> 

**1.)** Power the printer off and gently remove the jammed paper from the feeder.

**2.)** Open access panels to ensure there aren't any paper scraps inside.

**3.)** Gently reseat and reload the paper properly into the tray.

**4.)** Check the rollers for dust buildup or debris and clean with a clean cloth.

**5.)** Avoid overload or overfilling the paper tray and always use the recommended paper sizing.
